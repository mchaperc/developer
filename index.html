The Iron Yard final project

This project was the culmination of my time and the skills I learned while at The Iron Yard. We are each given 3 weeks to design and implement a project of our choice. I chose to build a real estate application (despite serious difficulties obtaining MLS data - I was able to get a beta sampling of data from Houston to suffice for the proof of concept this represents). Unlike other real estate applications, my goal was to expand the amount of information offered to a user. It's common practice to list the local school information, but I wanted to go a step farther with FBI crime data. Unfortunately, several of the APIs utilized in this project are now down, but the general idea is still in tact.

The following technologies were utilized in the building of this project:

  • - Backbone
  • - Node/Express/Axios (utilized as a proxy server to compile API resources and package them neatly before returning to client)
  • - Underscore
  • - Handlebars
  • - Google Maps
  • - SCSS/Bourbon/Neat
  • - jQuery
  • - Gulp
  • - Parse
  • - Highcharts

About me

Originally a classically trained musician, I began seeking an alternative path through which I could channel my passion for creativity. I spent 11 years teaching and writing music for high school, collegiate, and independent ensembles, all of which taught me many lessons about the creative process, organization, and leadership.

A little more than 2 years ago, I made the decision to pursue a passion I'd forgotten about since high school when I made toy games with friends in my abundant spare time: coding. I spent a year in self study before enrolling in The Iron Yard, where I graduated in July, 2015. A week after graduation, I began my new career, professionally, as a UI Engineer with BenefitFocus in Greenville, SC.

In November of 2015, a colleague - Dan Shapiro - and I founded Software Juice, LLC. We have our first customer's launch approaching in July, so stay tuned for that and many more projects to come. You can check out the placeholder here: Komposium
